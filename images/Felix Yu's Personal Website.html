Felix Yu

Hi! I'm Felix, a senior at the University of Michigan studying Computer Science and Earth Science.

About Me

I'm originally from Fort Collins, Colorado. As an outdoor enthusiast, I enjoy skiing, surfing, hiking, and staying active. I'm interested in developing technology to help our climate in various sectors like renewables, carbon sequestration, reforestration, and ocean technology. I enjoy building stories with data and have used machine learning in a variety of ways working at various labs. Check out my projects and work experience pages to learn more!

Current Work

Machine Learning Analysis of LiDAR Wetlands Data

Developing novel machine learning methods to analyze full-waveform LiDAR data flown from the National Ecological Observatory to better estimate wetland carbon biomass stocks.

Neural Network Analysis of Snow Albedo Data

Programmed a convolutional neural network using Tensorflow and Python to downscale GOES-R satellite Albedo data and images to improve snowmelt forecasting. My work supported an AGU 2024 paper called "Observations of super-unit albedo in complex terrain".

Biodiversity Analysis of National Parks

Evaluated species biodiversity data in US National Parks using Matplotlib and Pandas, flagging certain parks with high threatened species levels.
